# Pre-registration: §v2.4-proxy — input-distribution shift

**Status:** QUEUED · target commit `80d5320` · 2026-04-15

Derived from [docs/chem-tape/experiments-v2.md §v2.4-proxy](../docs/chem-tape/experiments-v2.md).

## Question (one sentence)

Is the §v2.4 F_AND failure specifically caused by the `max > 5`
proxy-predicate attractor, or will evolution find whatever proxy the input
distribution provides even after this one is removed?

## Hypothesis

Under [0,9], `max > 5` is a ~0.92-fitness proxy for `sum>10 AND max>5` (per
§v2.4 direct genotype inspection). Under [0,5], `max > 5` is structurally
impossible, so the label function collapses to `sum > 10 AND False = False`.
However, **the task builder must generate balanced training data, which
requires positive examples**. To keep the "same label, different
distribution" design intact, positives are generated by a small sampler
extension that draws the AND-true subset via an auxiliary rule (see
"Degenerate-success guard" — this is a design wrinkle worth flagging
explicitly). With that in place: if F_AND rises sharply on [0,5], the
original attractor was the specific barrier. If F_AND remains near zero,
evolution finds a different proxy and the attractor story generalises.

## Degenerate-success guard (required)

**Two concerns, both pre-committed to here before running:**

**(1) Decorrelating `max > 5` from the label.** The original doc-draft design
(all-negatives under [0,5]) causes label-collapse (no positives). A naive
hybrid (positives from [0,9], negatives from [0,5]) makes `max > 5` a
**perfect** classifier (worse than original), not a weakened one. The
scientifically clean design is a **four-way stratified balanced sampler**
over the single distribution [0,9], enforcing equal-size cohorts across
the 2×2 of (label, max>5):

  - `+ / max>5`: `sum>10 AND max>5` (original positive class)
  - `+ / max≤5`: `sum>10 AND max≤5` (requires all cells ≤5 with sum>10
    — feasible, e.g., (5,5,5,3), (5,4,4,4))
  - `− / max>5`: `sum≤10 AND max>5` (max>5 but sum doesn't cross)
  - `− / max≤5`: `sum≤10 AND max≤5` (both conjuncts false)

Under this sampling, `P(max>5 | +) = P(max>5 | −) = 0.5` on training —
the proxy is fully decorrelated from the label. Learning the AND requires
learning **both** conjuncts; neither alone is a >0.50 predictor. Compare to
§v2.4's original training distribution where `max>5` was a ~0.92 predictor
due to the [0,9] base rates.

**Pre-commitment:** use the **four-way stratified sampler**. A PASS means
"when the max>5 proxy is decorrelated, evolution can find the AND body."
A FAIL means "even a decorrelated proxy doesn't unblock search — the
compositional search landscape under this decoder/budget is structurally
hard." This is a substantive revision of the doc draft, accepted by the
user on 2026-04-15.

**(2) Trivial-predicate attractor under (b).** With the hybrid sampler, a new
proxy becomes available: "all cells ≤ 5" (true on all-negative samples
drawn from [0,5], false on positives drawn from [0,9]). This could itself
become an attractor with ~0.50–0.75 fitness. Detect via: genotype inspection
on any ≥0.90-fitness seed; check whether the program encodes a range-check
rather than true AND composition.

**Inspection commitment:** on any outcome ≥ 10/20 solves, decode the top 5
best-of-run genotypes. Report attractor category (max>5 / range-check /
genuine-AND / other) to the chronicle.

## Scope tag

**If this experiment passes, the claim enters findings.md scoped as:**
`within-family / n=20 / at pop=1024 gens=1500 BP_TOPK(k=3,bp=0.5) /
on sum_gt_10_AND_max_gt_5 with four-way stratified sampling decorrelating
max>5 from the label (from ~0.92 predictor → 0.50 predictor)`

Explicitly **not** claiming: "mechanism handles AND composition broadly,"
"distribution shift fixes compositional search in general," or "§v2.4 was
wrong." A pass is specifically evidence that *weakening the max>5 proxy
signal* is sufficient to unblock search on *this specific label*.

## Decision rule

- **PASS-attractor-specific →** §v2.4's failure is attractor-specific.
  §v2.4 verdict narrows: "on the original [0,9] distribution, the max>5
  attractor was the blocker; weakening the proxy signal unblocks search."
  Compositional-depth scaling is **still not demonstrated** — §v2.4-alt
  is the experiment that does that.
- **PASS-partial →** report; inspect non-solvers.
- **INCONCLUSIVE →** report as-is; no scheduled follow-up.
- **FAIL →** proxy-basin attractor framing is general. §v2.4's structural
  read stands. Mechanism is more limited than the doc's §v2.4 follow-up
  framing conceded.
